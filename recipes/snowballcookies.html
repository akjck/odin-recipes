Snowball Cookies

Description

A favorite recipe of my grandma's. A shortbread-like cookie with chocolate chips. Perfect for a cold, Christmassy day.

Unfortunately, I could not remeber the exact recipe so this one is from cookingclassy.com.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup sliced almonds
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3/8 tsp salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar (can substitute with regular sugar)
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (or more) chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Grind almonds in a food processor to tiny bits. In a mixing bowl whisk together flour and salt.
  2. Cream together butter and 1/2 cup powdered sugar until combined, just blended and slightly creamy.
  3. Mix in vanilla, then with mixer set on low speed slowly add in flour and salt mixture and mix just until combined.
  4. Add in chocolate chips and almonds and mix to evenly distribute.
  5. Scoop dough out 1 Tbsp at a time and roll into a ball. Transfer dough balls to an ungreased cookie sheet.
  6. Bake in preheated oven 10 - 12 minutes until bottoms are lightly golden brown. Let cool for just a few minutes, then pour remaining 1 cup powdered sugar into a small mixing bowl.
  7. Dip and roll cookies in powdered sugar to get a light coating then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Once cookies are cool roll again in powdered sugar, this time giving them a thicker coating. Store cookies in an airtight container.
